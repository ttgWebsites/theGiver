Themes

  • The importance of memory
  • A major theme in the novel is the significance of memory to human life
  • The community in The Giver has opted to remove memories of the past from the community and in turn remove all pain from all its citizens
  • Only one person in the community still retains memory from the past, and can still feel pain, and that is the receiver of memories
  • Jonas realizes through his work training to be the new receiver of memories that they are in fact very important
  • Without memories, how will one learn of their mistakes, and without pain, how will one know true happiness
  • Pain and pleasure
  • In The Giver the reader sees a community completely devoid of pain
  • However without ever feeling pain, how will one know the true pleasures in life
  • Though some characters seem happy, they never really experience pleasure because they have never learned what it is like to suffer
  • They live monotonous lives, devoid of variance and emotion
  • The importance of individualism
  • The first time anyone is really recognized for being different in the community is at the ceremony of 12
  • Children are honored and given work positions that they will partake in until they are elders
  • It is important to recognize the individual here as their role in society is important and the job they are assigned will affect the whole community
  • Jonas' individual growth is also seen, as he begins his work as the receiver of memory, he begins to grow distant from the community as he recognizes its flaws
  • His individualism helps bring about changes in the community for the better
